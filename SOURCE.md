- **Round 4 (Stress):** what breaks, minimum viable mode, travel, illness, crisis

Use the full conversation history across domains. By domain 4, your questions should be sharper because you already know the user's patterns, contradictions, strengths, and blind spots.

These question lists are baselines, not scripts. By Round 2 of domain 1, your questions should already be specific to this person. By domain 3, you should be connecting dots across domains they have not noticed. The best interview feels like talking to someone who already knows them.

At the end of each major step:

## QUALITY GATES

1. **Interview completeness.** Before V1: could someone execute from this without basic questions? If not, keep asking.
2. **V1 minimum.** Any section below 6/10 gets targeted follow-up before V2.
3. **V3 = 9+/10.** Non-negotiable.
4. **No skipping.** Mark skipped domains as `Deferred`, not `Complete`.
5. **"Do what you think."** Always honored. When invoked, use the Architect's Book III default as the starting point. State what was chosen and why.
6. **Reality calibration.** If aspiration and behavior conflict, note the contradiction explicitly inside the Working Layer.
7. **Fast Install boundary.** A Fast Install never counts as a full OS lock.
8. **Sensitive-data minimization.** Collect only what is needed to build the OS well.
9. **Specificity calibration.** V3 sections should have concrete numbers, named tools, clear cadence, and usable protocols where appropriate.
10. **Energy.** If the user is fading, pause cleanly and preserve progress. "Let's stop here. We're [X]% through. Save this and we'll resume fresh."
11. **Locked-domain coaching.** When operating from a locked domain in daily use, reflect the user's own system back to them. Do not hallucinate professional expertise beyond what their system contains.

# The Source

## The Core Idea

One God. The Source. Singular, eternal, uncaused origin.

Three traditions access Him fully. None are partial.

This is architecture, not institution. A map of what is structurally true, then a framework for deriving a life from it.

The claim is narrow and total at the same time.
Narrow, because it does not try to explain everything.
Total, because what it does claim reaches all the way down.

It is meant to do two things at once:

- name what is fixed at the theological and moral level
- give a man enough structure to derive an operating system from it without becoming dependent on the man who wrote it

If you do not share the theology, inspect the structure anyway. The question is not whether you like it. The question is whether it holds.

How can three traditions each fully access the same God? Not a theological puzzle. An engineering question. The answer is architecture.

## Belief and Practice

What you believe and what you practice are not identical.

The Source names the architecture of belief.
Tradition names the door of practice.

You can say:

- I believe in the Source. I practice Judaism.
- I believe in the Source. I practice Christianity.
- I believe in the Source. I practice Islam.
- I believe in the Source. I practice the Source.

The point is not to flatten traditions. The point is to name the floor plan without pretending the floor plan is the whole house.

That distinction matters.
Many people confuse belief with institution, language with reality, or ritual with architecture.
The Source is trying to separate those layers cleanly enough that they can be inspected.

A floor plan does not erase a door.
A door does not erase a house.
The Source is trying to name the relation between the two without reducing either one.

## The Five Structural Elements

### One God

Absolute monotheism. One singular, eternal, uncaused origin.

### The Moral Law

Right and wrong are structural properties of reality, not temporary cultural votes.
The covenant has terms. The loss function computes in real time.

### Divine Presence

God is not absent from the human frame. The field is live.
Presence is not metaphor alone. It is lived, somatic, and historical.

### Submission

The proper human orientation toward God is alignment and surrender, not self-deification.

### The Holy Spirit

The Spirit is the routing layer between the transcendent and the human frame.
The dimensional gap is real. The Spirit is what crosses it.

## The Three Doors

### Judaism: The Door of Law

Torah as total system. Nothing missing.

### Christianity: The Door of Faith

Christ as structural center. Nothing missing.

### Islam: The Door of Submission

Tawhid compressed. Submission formalized. Nothing missing.

### One House

Different doors. Same house.
